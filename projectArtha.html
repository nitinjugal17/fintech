Project Artha: Overview & Business Case

This document outlines the project plan for "Project Artha," a new platform feature enabling SEBI-certified advisors to create and manage investment pools for retail investors. The project's primary objective is to develop a secure, transparent, and compliant ecosystem that captures a new market segment and enhances user engagement. The business case is predicated on democratizing access to expert financial management, thereby increasing platform transaction volume and assets under management (AUM).

Core Project Objectives & Success Metrics:

Objective 1: Regulatory Compliance

Build a platform that achieves 100% adherence to all SEBI guidelines for investment advisors and collective investment schemes.

KPI: Zero compliance infractions in biannual audits.

Objective 2: Market Adoption

Achieve significant uptake from both certified advisors and retail investors within the first year of launch.

KPI: Onboard 500+ advisors and 50,000+ investors in Year 1.

Objective 3: System Security

Implement a robust, AI-driven fraud detection system to ensure the integrity of all transactions and protect user assets.

KPI: >95% accuracy in flagging anomalous transactions.

Technical Architecture & Implementation Timeline

The project will be executed in four distinct phases, starting with an MVP to validate the core concept and progressively adding features. The architecture is designed for scalability, security, and real-time performance, utilizing a microservices approach to ensure modularity and ease of maintenance.

Proposed Tech Stack

Frontend

React/Next.js, Chart.js, Tailwind CSS

Backend

Node.js (NestJS), Python (Flask), gRPC

Database

PostgreSQL (Transactional), ClickHouse (Analytics)

DevOps & Infra

Docker, Kubernetes, AWS/GCP, Kafka

Project Implementation Timeline

Phase 1: MVP Development (3 Months)

Core functionalities: Advisor onboarding, pool creation, investor joining, basic trade execution.

Phase 2: Alpha/Beta Testing (1 Month)

Internal testing followed by a closed beta with a select group of advisors and investors.

Phase 3: Fraud Engine Integration (2 Months)

Develop and integrate the AI/ML model for real-time transaction monitoring.

Phase 4: Public Launch & Scaling (Ongoing)

Official launch, performance monitoring, and iterative feature enhancements based on user feedback.

Regulatory Hurdles & The Path Forward

Innovation often precedes regulation. Project Artha is designed to operate within a potential future-state regulatory framework. This analysis deconstructs current regulatory hurdles and proposes how technology-driven solutions can address the core principles behind these rules, justifying a re-evaluation.

Core Regulatory Hurdles (Current State)

1. Collective Investment Scheme (CIS) Regulations

Current CIS rules are designed for large-scale funds, imposing heavy compliance and structural costs that are prohibitive for smaller, more agile advisor-led pools.

2. Investment Advisor (IA) Fee Structure

SEBI regulations cap the fees IAs can charge and place restrictions on performance-based fees to prevent conflicts of interest and mis-selling.

3. Segregation of Advisory & Execution

The framework traditionally separates the roles of advising on investments and executing trades to ensure advice is impartial and not influenced by transaction commissions.

Project Artha's Proposed Solution (Future State)

1. A Tech-Enabled "Micro-CIS" Framework

We propose a new regulatory category for ALPs (Advisor-Led Pools) where technology provides oversight. Automated compliance checks, transparent dashboards, and real-time monitoring can fulfill the investor protection spirit of CIS rules at a fraction of the cost.

2. Transparent, Pre-Agreed Performance Fees

The conflict of interest is mitigated when performance fees are calculated by an impartial platform, agreed upon by the investor beforehand with full disclosure, and are based on net profits. This aligns advisor and investor interests perfectly.

3. Platform as a Fiduciary Executor

In this model, the advisor's role remains purely advisory. The platform acts as the execution agent, operating on the pre-authorized instructions of the investor who joins the pool. This maintains the principle of separation, with technology ensuring the advisor cannot deviate from the pool's stated strategy.

Risk Mitigation: AI-Powered Fraud Detection

A key project risk is the potential for platform misuse, including market manipulation or collusion. Our primary mitigation strategy is an AI-powered monitoring system. This system establishes a baseline of normal trading behavior for each advisor and flags significant deviations in real-time. The chart below visualizes a simulated detection event.

Risk Register

Risk: Market Manipulation

Advisor rapidly buying/selling illiquid stock to inflate its price.

Mitigation: Volume & velocity spike detection.

Risk: Front-Running

Advisor makes personal trades before executing a large pool trade.

Mitigation: Cross-account pattern analysis.

Risk: Collusion

Multiple pools coordinating trades to influence the market.

Mitigation: Inter-pool correlation monitoring.

Project Roadmap & Success Metrics (KPIs)

Following the initial launch, the project will transition into an iterative development cycle focused on growth and feature enhancement. Success will be measured against a clear set of Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) tracked on a monthly and quarterly basis.

Post-Launch Roadmap

Q1 Post-Launch

Advanced Analytics Dashboard

Launch a comprehensive dashboard for advisors with portfolio attribution analysis and performance metrics.

Q2 Post-Launch

Native Mobile Application

Develop and release dedicated iOS and Android apps for an improved mobile user experience.

Q4 Post-Launch

Integration with Thematic Baskets

Allow advisors to create pools based on pre-defined thematic investment baskets (e.g., ESG, Tech, EV).

Key Performance Indicators

  • User Growth: Monthly active investors & onboarded advisors.
  • Financial: Total Assets Under Management (AUM) in pools.
  • Engagement: Average investment amount per user.
  • Platform Health: System uptime and transaction processing speed.
  • Risk Management: False positive rate of the fraud detection engine.
