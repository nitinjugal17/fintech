Navigating Regulation: Hurdles & Artha's Solutions

Project Artha redefines compliance by leveraging technology to uphold the *spirit* of SEBI regulations, rather than being constrained by the *letter* of outdated rules. We propose a collaborative path through a regulatory sandbox.

Hurdle 1: Collective Investment Scheme (CIS) Regulations

Current CIS rules impose heavy compliance costs (‚Çπ50 Cr net worth, trustee appointment) designed for large funds, making smaller advisor-led pools unviable. This is a barrier to entry for democratized wealth management.

Artha's Solution: Digital Trustee & Real-Time Transparency

The platform acts as a **Digital Trustee**, using segregated digital accounts and immutable audit trails for asset segregation and granular reporting. Real-time dashboards replace static prospectuses, offering superior, continuous investor disclosure.

Hurdle 2: Investment Advisor (IA) Fee Structure

SEBI caps IA fees and heavily restricts performance-based compensation to prevent conflicts of interest and mis-selling. This limits aligned incentives crucial for a dynamic, results-driven model.

Artha's Solution: Platform-Enforced Smart Contracts

Fees are calculated and distributed automatically by the **impartial platform** based on pre-agreed terms and *net profits*. This 'smart contract' model removes the advisor's ability to manipulate compensation, ensuring perfect alignment of interests.

Hurdle 3: Segregation of Advisory & Execution Roles

Regulations strictly separate advising from trade execution to ensure impartial advice, preventing brokers from influencing advice for commissions. The ALP model, where advisors also manage trades, appears to blur these lines.

Artha's Solution: Advisor as Strategist, Platform as Fiduciary Executor

The advisor's role is purely strategic and managerial. The **platform acts as the Fiduciary Executor**, executing trades based on the advisor's instructions within the constraints of the pool's disclosed strategy. This tech-mediated separation maintains the purity of advice.
